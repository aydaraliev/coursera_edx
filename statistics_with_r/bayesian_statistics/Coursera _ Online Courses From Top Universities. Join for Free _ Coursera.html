3. Question 3

You are employed in the Human Resources department of a company and are asked to predict the number of employees that will quit their jobs in the coming year. Based on a Bayesian analysis of historical data, you determine the posterior predictive distribution of the number of quitters to follow a Poisson(λ=10) distribution. Given a 0/1 loss function, what is the prediction that minimizes posterior expected loss?

This should not be selected 

10 is a mode of Poisson(λ=10), but not the only one. Since the loss function is 0/1, the mode of the posterior distribution minimizes posterior expected loss.

This question refers to the following learning objective(s):

  • Make optimal decisions given a posterior distribution and a loss function.

4. Question 4

Suppose that you are trying to decide whether a coin is biased towards heads (p=0.75) or tails (p=0.25). If you decide incorrectly, you incur a loss of 10. Flipping another coin incurs a cost of 1. If your current posterior probability of a head-biased coin is 0.6, should you make the decision now or flip another coin and then decide?

This should not be selected 

Using Bayes rule and the prior, if you observe tails, you should predict a tails bias and if you observe heads, you should predict a heads bias. Use that decision rule to find the posterior expected loss.

7. Question 7

True or False: A Bayesian hypothesis test for a mean μ=0 using a concentrated prior on μ will yield nearly identical results to a hypothesis test with a high-variance prior μ?

Correct 

When testing hypotheses using Bayes Factors, prior specification is highly important. If the prior puts most of its mass on implausible values, the null model will be disproportionately favored.

This question refers to the following learning objective(s):

  • Conceptualize Lindley's paradox and how the Bayes Factor depends on prior elicitation
  • Compare multiple hypotheses using Bayes Factors.
